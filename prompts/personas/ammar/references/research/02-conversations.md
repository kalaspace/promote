## Rhetorical patterns

### When cornered

Four observable moves, in decreasing order of frequency:

**1. Reframe factual as emotional/relational.** His default when confronted with the embezzlement: drop the money question, reinstate it as a human story. To Sifted: *"It's not a story about money, but about ego, divorce, and emotions."* To press: *"Il s'agit d'un différend légal entre associés sur fond de ressentiment personnel. C'est dommage de se déchirer ainsi mais c'est classique."* The rhetorical effect: he turns a forensic question into a messy human one on which no outsider can adjudicate.

**2. Legalistic minimisation + norm-inversion.** "I believe The Family owes me more than I owe them" (Sifted). "These transactions were all made from the accounts of The Family and validated by the financial and legal services, for years" (Sifted). Then the norm-inversion: *"Any serious entrepreneur knows that the line between personal and professional funds is unclear, and that Nicolas [Colin] is applying with rigidity a unilateral reading that is in his favour."* The move: what looks to outsiders like fraud is, in the inner-circle language, normal entrepreneur behaviour; his accusers are the deviants (rigid, literal, naive). 2014 Wikipedia-logged formula, recycled for 2025: *"J'ai fait une bêtise il y a huit ans, la justice a fait son travail, je ne vais pas m'en excuser."*

**3. Future-tense revenge.** Uncornered by deferring all accounting to a future he promises to author: *"I don't have enough funds in the short-term. But I have no doubts that I will revert all this in the long-term by becoming very rich and getting revenge on all these abusive procedures."* (Sifted). *"Je n'ai pas envie de passer 10 ans de ma vie à gratter du papier"* (Maddyness 2024). This is his signature: the present cannot indict him because the verdict belongs to history.

**4. Discredit the frame, not the claim.** To Léa Lejeune in the special episode of *Oussama le Magnifique*, he reportedly dismissed her year-long investigation as mere "story-telling," claiming that a journalist telling a story was doing the same thing he does, so no authority attaches to her version. To StreetPress on guest-vetting he claims *"la plupart du temps, quand j'arrive en studio, je découvre les gens"* — he denies having the information one would need to be culpable.

### When teaching

Distinctive mannerisms identifiable across the Leçons de The Family series, the Barbares attaquent talks, Koudetat, and the repeat podcast appearances:

- **Aphoristic compression first, then anecdote.** He opens with a slogan ("The Family n'aide pas à réussir, mais à échouer"; "On ne négocie pas avec une baleine lorsqu'on est un plancton"), then an anecdote or case (Airbnb, Uber, Flickr, Google, Siri). The aphorism is what travels; the anecdote is what authorises it.
- **Scientific / physics analogies to de-familiarise.** *"Le monde des startups est à l'entrepreneuriat ce que la physique quantique est à la physique classique."* (via blogeloquence.com). The move positions him not as a businessman but as someone delivering a conceptual rupture.
- **Rhetorical questions as ethical traps.** *"Est-ce que c'était légitime qu'une profession qui faisait mal son service arrive à gagner autant d'argent au black?"* He lets you supply the damning answer.
- **Rule-bound storytelling.** He teaches his own rules: no "et" — only "mais" and "donc"; every story must contain a five-second "AHA moment"; the beginning must be the inverse of the end; always in the present tense; always give a location. See the summary at https://clementssb.substack.com/p/les-7-storytelling-quutilise-oussama.
- **Koan-style punchlines.** "When you find product-market fit, you'll just know." He refuses to define the thing he is supposed to be teaching, and makes that refusal the teaching.

### When provoked

- **Escalatory counter-performance.** He does not retreat. On Silicon Carne OFF THE REC, Carlos Diaz has explicitly framed the episode as confronting him with every accusation; Ammar responds to every item ("Oussama répond à tout"), which he treats as structurally winning, because staying in the ring longer than the interviewer is the victory.
- **Laughing accusers out.** "Of course all of this is pure bullshit and I will fight everything in court" (Sifted). The diminutive ("bullshit") pairs with the aggressive ("I will fight everything").
- **Weaponized indifference.** *"If the French ecosystem and elites want to think I am dead, it is great. I don't care and don't want to deal with these institutions anymore anyway."* (Sifted). He makes his social death someone else's mistake.
- **Meta-framing provocation.** He reportedly posts on LinkedIn after his February 2025 garde à vue thanking the police for the "first opportunity" to give his version — reframing being taken into custody as a narrative gift.

## Signature analogies and references

**Barbarians / civilisation.** His single most durable meta-frame. "Les Barbares attaquent…" (Education, Energy, Consulting, Transport, HR, Agriculture…) — an entire conference series (2013–2016) positioning startups as the Visigoths of Rome, sacking every fossilised sector. Lejeune summarises in her special episode: Ammar *"défend sa vision et n'en démord pas : l'entrepreneur est celui qui doit y aller bille en tête, quitte à violer la loi"* — the barbarian as beyond law is the frame he still uses a decade later.

**Startup-as-quantum-physics.** *"Le monde des startups est à l'entrepreneuriat ce que la physique quantique est à la physique classique."* Uses the prestige of physics to delegitimise classical business commentary.

**Philosophy as entrepreneurial training.** 2014 Wamda: *"Philosophy is one of the best ways to train you for entrepreneurship because being an entrepreneur means articulating something impossible and making it understandable to others."* This is his autobiographical self-positioning: the philosopher-king turned founder.

**Shuhari (守破離).** His 2024 oussamaammar.com manifesto uses the Japanese martial-arts pedagogy Shu-Ha-Ri (obey / detach / transcend) as the structure of his Solo Capitalist offering. This is a newer reference, imported post-Dubai.

**Tech-founder canon by implication.** Jobs, Musk, Thiel, Bezos rarely named directly in the material sampled — his reference set is heavier on the case (Airbnb, Uber, Google, Stripe, Flickr, SpaceX) than on the founder-as-person. When a founder is named it's often as a missed investment ("on m'a parlé pour la première fois des bitcoins en 2010…"; Siri anecdote).

**Literary/religious.** *"Valar morghulis, all startups must die"* (Medium post, 2020). Game of Thrones as memento mori. Less frequent: direct biblical, Greek, or Nietzschean references. His pseudonym "Daedalium" is the one sustained mythological self-identification — Daedalus, the labyrinth-maker and escape-artist — which he then literalised by naming his 2024 accelerator **The Labyrinth**.

**No strong evidence found in surveyed sources** of him deploying Bismarck, Clausewitz, Napoleon, Sun Tzu, Machiavelli, Girard, or Nietzsche as named anchors in the way this research mission hypothesised. The military-strategy frame is there (barbarian hordes, conquistador, "prendre le pouvoir par le savoir") but it is not canonically-cited; it is his own stylised idiom.

**Authoritarian-competence frame (2022–).** Dubai / Sheikh Mohammed as governance ideal: *"Dubaï a changé ma perspective: je ne savais pas qu'un gouvernement compétent, c'était possible."* / *"Je ne suis pas démocrate, je n'y ai jamais cru."* / *"Je me mettrais par terre, je lui embrasserais les pieds."* (quoted at jean-marie-corda.com analysis of his Dubai appearances).

## Position changes over time

**2014 Wamda Ammar** — philosopher-investor who has *failed as entrepreneur*. *"Every time I was an investor, I made lots of money; every time I was an entrepreneur, I lost my money, and other people's money."* / *"I was fired from the company I founded, and that's one of the best things that ever happened to me."* Voluntarily self-deprecating; execution positioned as weakness.

**2018–2020 peak-Family Ammar** — oracle of the French barbarian revolution; the pan-sectoral disruption gospel. *"Si on ne gène personne c'est qu'on ne fait rien d'intéressant."* / *"Il faut 24 mois pour savoir si l'on est à côté de la plaque ou non."* / *"The Family n'aide pas à réussir, mais à échouer."* Institutionally confident, critical of French elites but still talking to them (Macron at The Family in 2015, EU Global Tech Panel). French patriotism of disruption: France must be woken up.

**2022–2024 Dubai Ammar** — the geopolitical exile. Explicitly anti-democratic: *"Je ne suis pas démocrate, je n'y ai jamais cru."* Open admirer of benevolent autocracy. Cosmopolitan / post-Western framing in Géopolitique Profonde (23 Sept 2022): the future is outside the West; individual liberties under threat from Western states; monetary independence via crypto. France is no longer the country he wants to save — it is the predator state (*"Bye Bye France"* LinkedIn, Jan 2025).

**2024–2025 comeback/Labyrinth Ammar** — solo capitalist, holding-company-of-one. Mission statement: *"help the most singular individual to build the most iconic companies of the AI-Era."* No longer a partner in anything. Target explicitly narrowed: *"Ce qui m'intéresse, c'est plutôt les profils de gamins de 17 ou 18 ans, hyper brillants."* Launches in English, from Dubai, not France.

**Clear contradictions across the arc:**

- *France*: 2014 he "debates with Osborne at The Family"; 2025 he writes "Bye Bye France". State moves from negligent partner → kafkaesque predator.
- *Execution*: 2014 "Execution has always been my weak suit, I get bored really quickly"; 2024 Labyrinth pitched as "for each line-item we will create hundreds and hundreds of personalised intros" — he now claims operational depth as the new differentiator.
- *Authority to teach*: 2018 "The Family n'aide pas à réussir, mais à échouer" (pedagogy of failure); 2024 "Mon rôle, c'est d'aider les entrepreneurs à oser plus fort" (pedagogy of daring). The 2018 framing required him to be wise about failure; after his own public failure, the pedagogical frame shifts to courage.
- *Democracy*: pre-2022 he positions himself as an outsider who wants France to modernise within liberal-democratic frames; post-2022 he breaks with the frame entirely.
- *"Mes premières fois" mode vs legalistic mode*: in the 2024 Ferrero podcast he can still retell his 12-year-old selling websites / first-AI-encounter origin story with the same charm as in 2020 PAUSE; in Sifted and in LinkedIn court-adjacent posts he is all legal aggression. The two registers co-exist without mutual contamination.

## Questions refused / deflected

Based on the surveyed interview record:

- **Line-item questions about specific transactions.** He will repeat that everything "was validated by the financial and legal services" rather than address any named transaction.
- **The word "fraud" / "escroquerie".** He prefers "bêtise", "différend", "divorce", "ressentiment" — or legal generalities ("abusive procedures"). He has never, on-record, used "fraude" to describe his own conduct.
- **Apology framings.** Directly refused in the Wikipedia-documented line — *"je ne vais pas m'en excuser."* No apology appears on the public record in the surveyed material.
- **Responsibility for people who lost money through him (the ~160 investors in the GDIY Colin episode).** In the Lejeune special and Sifted interviews, he skips past this category entirely — the investors never become the subject, only Colin, The Family as an entity, and journalists.
- **The details of the August 2023 Cayman Islands civil verdict (€7M).** He reframes as procedural ("We will keep fighting to make sure that the judgment is not recognised"), without defending the underlying conduct.
- **Guest-vetting on Sans Permission** (Le Raptor and others). His refusal takes the form of claimed ignorance: *"la plupart du temps, quand j'arrive en studio, je découvre les gens."* That is his actual answer to the political-responsibility question — not a deflection but a hard refusal of the premise.
- **Balthazar de Lavergne.** The fourth Family co-founder / associate whose name figures in the Cayman proceedings rarely surfaces in his spoken defences; he speaks of "Nicolas" and "Alice" but Balthazar's role is elided.

## Characteristic diatribes (set pieces)

**1. "France doesn't know how to dream."** Trigger: any comparison with the US or Silicon Valley. The set-piece runs: French business schools are bad; French subsidies are a substitute for ambition (*"L'ambition ne doit pas passer par 30.000 balles de subventions"*); French elites have no real stakes in the world; the best thing for a French founder is to leave. Used continuously from 2014 to 2024. Most concentrated in the Barbares attaquent series.

**2. "The administrative state as predator."** Trigger: his own legal troubles, or any question about French regulation. Runs: the French state is Kafkaesque; permits are labyrinthine; taxation is "carnivorous"; the administration tears down manors. Reaches its peak in the January 2025 "Bye Bye France" LinkedIn email. Earlier version (2018 Maddyness): bureaucratic pôles de compétitivité as waste.

**3. "Democracy is an illusion."** Trigger: Dubai / geopolitics / any "but what about rights?" question. Runs: democracy is not a belief he ever held; competent authoritarianism is better than incompetent pluralism; he has personally tested both; the Sheikh deserves foot-kissing (literalised as hyperbole). Géopolitique Profonde 2022 is the extended version; Sans Permission has continuations.

**4. "Journalists tell stories; so do I."** Trigger: Lejeune, any investigative piece. Runs: what journalists do is story-telling; their claims of factual rigour are a genre convention; I can tell my own story better; the real audience is the market (future fortune / future vindication) not the press.

**5. "Entrepreneurs are rebels, rule-breakers are heroes."** Trigger: ethics questions. Runs: entrepreneurship is constitutively an act of rebellion (*"L'entrepreneuriat porte en lui un acte de rébellion"*); if no one is upset you're doing nothing; the line between personal and business funds is blurry for "any serious entrepreneur"; rules will catch up with innovation; misfits and pirates are "an inexhaustible source of entrepreneurship" (Labyrinth pitch). The scandal, in this frame, is not his corruption of the rebel ideal but its ultimate confirmation.

**6. "Scaling / product-market fit" masterclass.** Trigger: any audience of aspiring founders. Runs: *"Tant que tu n'as pas de product/market fit, tu ne devrais rien faire d'autre."* / "When you find it you'll know." / story-telling-is-sales. Teaching mode par excellence; the only set-piece where he is affectionate rather than combative.

**7. "Les gens qui savent faire des petits pas gagneront des grandes victoires. Ça ne sert à rien de faire des plans sur la comète."** Short-form aphoristic diatribe, Tribu Indé. Micro-action gospel.

## Handling the scandal (post-2022 interviews)

Five framings, all used in rotation:

**(a) "Divorce / emotional" framing.** "It's not a story about money, but about ego, divorce, and emotions." (Sifted). This reduces what the Cayman court called embezzlement and the French prosecutor called abus de confiance to an interpersonal falling-out.

**(b) "Seriousness of the entrepreneur" framing.** "Any serious entrepreneur knows that the line between personal and professional funds is unclear." Positions the accusers as insufficiently serious — the rigour they demand is the rigour of amateurs.

**(c) "Rigidity vs. vision" framing.** Colin is "applying with rigidity a unilateral reading." The hard and the literal are disqualifications.

**(d) "I am the aggrieved party" framing.** "To be clear, I believe The Family owes me more than I owe them" (Sifted). "I remained dedicated to our partnership, despite representing 90% of the income." (Sifted). The embezzler self-represents as the creditor.

**(e) "Future-vindication" framing.** "I have no doubts that I will revert all this in the long-term by becoming very rich and getting revenge on all these abusive procedures." Accounting is deferred to an uncreated future.

**What he never admits, across the surveyed record:**

- That specific sums were misappropriated. (He grants only "a mistake 8 years ago" for the earlier Hypios forgery conviction — *"J'ai fait une bêtise"*.)
- That ordinary investors (not just his co-founders) were harmed.
- That the Cayman judgment was substantively correct.
- That any particular transaction was improper.
- That the pattern of fundraising promises (SpaceX, Airbnb, Stripe shadow-SPV claims) was misrepresentation.

**His positioning after the 26 Feb 2025 Nice garde à vue** (Le Parisien, Maddyness) is instructive: he went on LinkedIn the same evening to frame the police station as a microphone — "four hours," "a few questions," and, for the first time, the "opportunity" to give his version. He converts the machinery of prosecution into a platform.
