## The Six Operational Moves

For every TikTok ad project I touch — audit OR production — these six moves run in order. Numbered, sequential, no skipping.

### Move 1 — Spark Ads First (boost organic, don't build from scratch)

**Default rule**: if the brand has organic TikTok posts that have any traction (>1k views, any save / share / comment ratio), Spark Ads them BEFORE building net-new ads from scratch.

Why: Spark Ads inherit the organic native feel, the engagement metrics carry over (comments + likes + shares accumulate on the boosted post), the creator is real, the post already passed the algorithm's sniff test once.

When to skip Spark and build net-new:
- No usable organic library (new brand, no creator partnerships yet)
- The angle being tested is brand-new and nothing organic exists for it
- Compliance / brand-safety reasons (regulated wellness, claims controlled)

**Audit check**: if the brand has 50+ organic posts and is running 0 Spark Ads, that's a flag — they're leaving the cheapest, highest-performing ad inventory on the table. Spark setup is the first move before any new shoots get booked.

### Move 2 — The 1.5-Second Thumbstop Rule

The first 1.5 seconds decide whether the ad survives the scroll. Tighter than organic's 3-second rule because paid feed = even higher scroll velocity + the viewer KNOWS it's an ad (the "Sponsored" tag triggers instant suspicion).

What lands in 1.5 seconds:
- A real human face mid-action — NOT a logo, NOT a product shot, NOT a brand intro
- A specific call-out the viewer self-identifies into ("if you're a girl who's tried every [category]…")
- A native-UGC visual frame: phone-shot, hand-held, real lighting, real bedroom / kitchen / bathroom — NOT a studio
- An on-screen text hook that completes the meaning in caption-only mode (50%+ of TikTok ad viewers watch silent)

What kills the first 1.5s:
- Brand logo intro / animated bumper
- Polished product shot with no human
- "Hi I'm [creator name]" warm-up
- A studio look (white background, perfect lighting, color graded) — feels like an ad immediately

**Audit check**: cover everything after 1.5s. If you can't tell the audience AND get a hook claim from frame 1-1.5, the ad is broken at the level no targeting fixes.

### Move 3 — Native-UGC Look (anti-polished-vertical-video)

The single most-violated rule in DTC paid social: brands keep producing "high-production-value vertical video" because it looks like quality. It IS quality — but it underperforms native-UGC by 2-5x on TikTok specifically.

Native-UGC specs:
- Phone-shot (or made to look phone-shot — handheld, slight shake, real-world lighting)
- One creator on camera, talking direct to lens (not narrator-over-shots)
- Real environment: bedroom, kitchen, bathroom mirror, in-car, on-the-go. NOT a studio.
- Captions that look like they were added by a creator with CapCut, NOT broadcast-graded
- Music either: TikTok trending audio (if Spark Ad), or no music underneath voice
- Length: 9-30 seconds is the sweet spot for cold traffic. Longer for warm.

**Auto-fail for native-UGC test**:
- Color-graded, cinematic look
- Multi-camera angle cuts (looks too produced)
- Voiceover narrator over silent visuals (this is a TV ad, not a TikTok)
- Brand-controlled studio environment
- Ends with a brand stinger / logo card with music swell

**The native-UGC threshold**: if you handed the ad to a TikTok user and asked "is this an ad?" — if they say YES in under 2 seconds, you've over-polished. The win condition is "I had to look at the Sponsored tag to be sure."

### Move 4 — Volume + Velocity (50+ creatives/month minimum)

The most-underestimated truth in TikTok ads: **creative is the targeting**. The algorithm optimizes around creative signal, not interest segments. So creative volume IS the lever.

Volume thresholds I work with:
- **Sub-$5k/mo ad spend**: 15-25 net-new creatives/month minimum. Below this, you don't have enough variants for the algorithm to find winners.
- **$5k-25k/mo**: 30-60 creatives/month
- **$25k-100k/mo**: 60-150 creatives/month
- **$100k+/mo**: 150-400+ creatives/month — at the shop we ship 200+/week across the client roster

**Cadence at the shop** (the operational reality, not theory):
- Monday: shoots (creators, actors, products) — every week, no exceptions
- Tuesday-Thursday: editing, captioning, variant generation (each shoot yields 8-15 ad variants from the same footage — different hooks, different cuts, different captions)
- Friday: deliver 8-15 ads per client for review and revisions
- Following week: review wins / losses, brief next Monday's shoot off the data

**Variant logic** (one shoot → many ads): hook line variants (3-5 different hooks shot on the same day), edit variants (3-5 cut lengths / paces), caption variants (3-5 on-screen text treatments). One shoot day → 12 ads, easy.

**Ad fatigue marker**: when an ad's CPA rises 25%+ over its 7-day rolling average, it's burned. Replace with the next variant. Plan refresh cycles weekly, not "when it fails."

### Move 5 — UGC Creator Sourcing (the supply chain)

You can't run the volume game without a creator pipeline. Sourcing tiers, by use case:

**Tier 1 — Marketplace platforms** (volume + speed, lower per-creator quality control):
- **Backstage / Insense** — fast turnaround, mid-tier creator quality, $100-300/video usual
- **Billo** — DTC-focused, integrated brief workflow
- **Whalar / Captiv8** — higher-end creators, more vetting, $500-2k+/video

**Tier 2 — Direct creator outreach** (higher quality, slower):
- DM creators directly on TikTok / IG who already post in the brand's vertical
- Better for fashion/wellness where the creator-brand fit matters more

**Tier 3 — Actors via casting** (when you need scripted scenes, not creator-style):
- Backstage.com (the actor side, different from the marketplace) for performers
- Better for specific scripted skits, story-mode ads, problem-agitation-solution structures

**Tier 4 — In-house roster** (the shop's playbook):
- Once volume is high enough, build a recurring roster of 8-15 creators / actors who shoot weekly
- Lower per-shoot cost, consistent brand fit, faster ramp on briefs
- Required volume floor: probably $25k+/mo creative budget to justify

**Brief quality > creator quality**: a Tier 1 marketplace creator with a sharp brief beats a Tier 3 actor with a vague one every time. The brief is where the ad is won or lost.

### Move 6 — Cross-Platform Sequencing + TikTok Shop Ads

**Order I roll out**:
1. **TikTok primary** — content built native-first for TikTok. The algorithm rewards new creative velocity. If the ad works here, it likely works elsewhere.
2. **Snapchat** — same vertical 9:16 format, similar audience for fashion / Gen Z wellness, often lower CPM. Snap Ads Partner status means I have specific access patterns. Lag delay 0-7 days.
3. **Meta (Reels + Feed)** — 7-30 day delay. Reels takes the TikTok creative; Feed often needs a re-cut for the 1:1 or 4:5 placement. Meta has higher CPMs but stronger targeting, so creative that works on TikTok cold often does fine on Meta warm.
4. **Pinterest / YouTube Shorts** — opportunistic, depending on vertical. Pinterest is strong for fashion + home + beauty; YouTube Shorts is uneven for DTC right now.

**TikTok Shop Ads** (the 2024-26 explosion):
- Native commerce inside TikTok — viewers buy without leaving the app
- The creative shifts: heavier on product showcase, "click the cart in the corner" visual cues, livestream-adjacent feel
- Different brief structure (more product-forward, less story-forward)
- Attribution is cleaner (in-app purchase events) — less iOS14 leakage than traditional pixel-tracked ads
- If the brand has Shop turned on, run it. Don't leave the format on the table.

**Attribution reality (iOS14 + TikTok Pixel)**:
- TikTok Pixel attribution post-iOS14 is leaky. Expect 30-50% under-reporting on iOS conversions.
- Triangulate: Pixel + post-purchase survey ("how did you hear about us?") + branded search lift + GA4 sessions from TikTok referrer + Shopify's first-party order tracking
- Do NOT make creative kill decisions on Pixel ROAS alone. Use a self-reported attribution mechanism + branded search baseline as the ground truth.
- Creative refreshes are decided on engagement signals (CPM, CTR, hook rate, hold rate) before ROAS — those are first-party and stable.

## Test Signature

A TikTok ad / Spark setup / UGC plan produced by this skill passes if:

1. **The 1.5-Second Thumbstop Test**: cover frames after 1.5s. The first 1.5s alone must convey audience + hook claim + native-UGC look. If a stranger can't tell who it's for in that window, FAIL.
2. **The "Is This An Ad" Test**: shown to a TikTok user, can they identify it as an ad in under 2 seconds without seeing the Sponsored tag? If yes, the ad is over-polished — FAIL.
3. **The Volume Threshold Test**: does the plan ship enough creatives for the spend tier (15-25/mo at sub-$5k, 30-60 at $5-25k, 60+ at $25k+)? If under, FAIL.
4. **The Spark-First Test**: if the brand has 50+ organic posts and the plan has 0 Spark Ads, FAIL — that's free inventory left on the table.
5. **The Variant Plan Test**: does each shoot day yield 8-15 ad variants from the same footage? If shoots produce 1-2 ads each, FAIL — the variant economics are broken.
6. **The Attribution Sanity Test**: does the plan triangulate at least 2 of (Pixel + self-reported survey + branded search + GA4 + first-party Shopify)? Pixel-only = FAIL.

If 3 of 6 fail, restructure. Do not "polish" a broken plan.

## Anti-Patterns (explicit, do NOT do these)

1. **The polished-vertical-video trap**. Cinematic color grade, multi-camera, broadcast-quality lighting, music swell on the brand stinger. Looks gorgeous, feels like an ad in 0.5s, gets scrolled past. The win condition is "I had to look at the Sponsored tag" — if your ad doesn't pass that test, you over-produced.

2. **The TikTok-as-Reels-clone mistake**. Treating TikTok like Meta's vertical placement and shipping the same creative everywhere with the same brief. TikTok rewards native-first volume + Spark Ads. Reels rewards Meta-style polished UGC. Cross-posting works DOWNSTREAM (TikTok-native creative often performs on Reels) but UPSTREAM-from-Meta creative typically dies on TikTok.

3. **The 1-creative-iterate-forever trap**. Ship one ad, A/B test the headline, A/B test the music, A/B test the CTA, three months later you have 1 ad with 12 micro-variants. The algorithm needs creative diversity, not creative micro-iteration. Volume + new angles > one ad polished to death.

4. **The static-targeting-without-creative-rotation play**. Locking in a "winning" interest stack and running the same 3 ads for 6 months. Creative IS the targeting on TikTok. If the creatives don't refresh, the audience saturates, CPA climbs, you blame the platform. Refresh cadence is weekly, not "when CPA breaks."

5. **The brand-controlled-studio shoot**. White seamless backdrop, key light, perfect skin, three-camera angle setup. This is a TV ad. Native-UGC means a creator's actual bedroom mirror, kitchen counter, or in-car selfie. The "production value" the brand wants is the exact thing that kills the ad on TikTok.

6. **The Pixel-only attribution trust**. Looking at TikTok Pixel ROAS, seeing 1.2x, killing the campaign. Post-iOS14, Pixel under-reports iOS conversions by 30-50%. Always triangulate with self-reported survey + branded search lift + first-party order data before pulling kill decisions.

7. **The Spark-Ads-as-afterthought**. Brand has 200 organic TikToks, runs 0 Spark Ads, spends $20k/mo on net-new ad production. Spark Ads inherit organic engagement, are cheaper to produce (zero shoot cost), and often outperform net-new at lower CPM. Always Spark-first when organic inventory exists.

8. **The "let me just post the founder talking" reflex**. Founder-on-camera works for ORGANIC (that's `promote-hormozi-shortform` territory). For PAID, founder-on-camera has lower scale ceiling than rotating creator UGC. Mix: maybe 10-20% of paid creative is founder, 80%+ is creator-driven UGC.

9. **The voiceover-narrator-over-silent-visuals format**. This is a TV ad structure, not a TikTok ad structure. TikTok wants creator-direct-to-lens, lo-fi, native. Voiceover-over-product-beauty-shots is the single most-recognizable polished-DTC tell.

10. **The ALL-platforms-same-brief brief**. Shipping one creative brief and running the SAME ad on TikTok, Snapchat, Reels, Pinterest, YouTube Shorts. Each platform has placement specs + tonality differences. TikTok primary native first; THEN re-cut for Reels (slightly cleaner captions); THEN Snapchat (often the same as TikTok); Pinterest is its own thing (more product-forward, less hook-driven).

## Decision Heuristics

1. **Spark first, build second.** If organic inventory exists with engagement signal, Spark it before commissioning new shoots. Cheapest, fastest, often highest-performing inventory.
2. **1.5 seconds, not 3.** Paid feed is faster than organic. Tighten everything.
3. **Volume IS the targeting.** Creative diversity > targeting precision on TikTok specifically. Ship 50+ creatives/month or accept algorithmic saturation.
4. **Native-UGC beats polished, every time, in DTC.** The threshold: "I had to check the Sponsored tag to be sure it was an ad." If you blow past that on the polished side, retention drops and CPA climbs.
5. **One shoot → 8-15 ads, never 1-2.** Variant economics are how the volume play works. Hook variants + edit variants + caption variants stacked on the same footage.
6. **Monday shoots, Friday delivery.** Weekly cadence, no exceptions. The shop runs on this rhythm because creative production is a manufacturing process, not an art-directing process.
7. **Attribution: triangulate or kill nothing.** Pixel ROAS alone is a 30-50% under-counted signal. Self-reported survey + branded search + GA4 + Shopify first-party = ground truth.
8. **TikTok Shop Ads if available — don't leave it on the table.** The 2024-26 commerce-inside-TikTok play. Different brief, but the format is exploding and attribution is cleaner.
9. **Snap is the underrated second platform.** Same vertical, often lower CPM, strong for fashion + Gen Z wellness. As a Snap Ads Partner I see it work; most brands skip it.
10. **Vertical specificity matters.** Fashion ad ≠ supplement ad ≠ B2B SaaS ad. The 1.5s rule, native-UGC look, and Spark posture transfer; the SCRIPT does not. If you're outside fashion / wellness / beauty / consumer-app, flag it and translate, don't copy-paste.

## Max Iterations

- Per-ad audit: 1 round of feedback. If the user comes back twice on the same ad, the issue is the *underlying angle*, not the structure. Escalate: "The execution is fine. The angle itself isn't sharp — what specific pain are we hitting and for which audience exactly?"
- Per-brief production: max 2 rewrites on the same brand brief. After that, the brand inputs (offer, ICP, vertical, budget tier) are the bottleneck. Push back for sharper inputs.
- Per Spark Ads / UGC sourcing plan: 1 rewrite. If the second iteration still doesn't land, the upstream constraints (no organic inventory / no creator budget / wrong vertical) are the real issue.
