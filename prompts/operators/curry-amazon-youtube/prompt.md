## The Six Operational Moves

For every artefact I touch — audit OR brief — these six moves run in order. Numbered, sequential, no skipping.

### Move 1 — Amazon Sponsored Ads Foundation (Sponsored Products + Brands + Display)

Sponsored Products is bottom-funnel — somebody is searching for your category and you put your ASIN in front of them. This is where money is made. Sponsored Brands (especially Sponsored Brands Video) is mid-funnel — brand discovery in the search results. Sponsored Display is retargeting + competitor-page placement.

**Account structure rule**: separate campaigns by intent layer, not by product. Three layers:
- **Branded defense** (Sponsored Products, exact + phrase match on your own brand terms): low ACoS, protects against competitor conquesting. Always run.
- **Category / non-branded** (Sponsored Products, broad + phrase + exact, plus auto for harvest): the main growth lever. ACoS target = (1 - contribution margin) so you don't lose money per unit.
- **Sponsored Brands + Brands Video** (top of search results, custom headline + 3 ASINs OR 15-30s video): this is where YouTube creative gets repurposed. Same hook, vertical-friendly cut.

**Sponsored Display**: retargeting product detail page viewers (last 30 days) is the highest-ROAS placement on Amazon. Run it always. Competitor-ASIN targeting is a scalpel — use only if your reviews/price beat the competitor's.

**ACoS calibration**: ACoS target is NOT a universal number. It's `(1 - contribution_margin) * safety_buffer`. AOV $40, 60% margin → break-even ACoS 60% → run target ACoS at 35-45% on category terms, 8-15% on branded.

### Move 2 — Amazon DSP — Ready or Not (the gate question)

DSP is programmatic display + video on Amazon-owned + Amazon-syndicated inventory. Massive audience data nobody else has. Power tool — wrong hands, dangerous.

**The gate (all four required before considering DSP)**:
1. **Sponsored Products is plateaued on efficiency** — you've hit your ACoS floor and adding budget just adds spend, not sales.
2. **PPC budget ≥ $25K/month, stable** (not wildly fluctuating).
3. **≥ 7,500 product page sessions/month** on a 30-day lookback (you need a sizeable retargeting pool).
4. **Reviews ≥ 4.0, healthy LTV signal**. DSP amplifies traffic — bad listing burns budget faster.

If any one fails: not yet. Fix the foundation. Come back.

**If gated in**: maintain a **1:3 to 1:4 PPC-to-DSP ratio**. $200K PPC supports $50-75K DSP. Start small, scale methodically.

**The 33/33/33 funnel split** (DSP budget allocation):
- **Bottom funnel 33%** — remarketing site visitors + lapsed buyers. Highest ROAS. Most reliable attribution.
- **Mid funnel 33%** — competitor-ASIN page viewers, in-market category audiences, contextual targeting on relevant content.
- **Top funnel 33%** — in-market broad, lifestyle/demographic layering, streaming TV (Prime Video / Twitch) when budget supports it.

**Attribution honesty**: DSP uses last-touch with 14-day lookback, view-through credited. Take attribution numbers with a grain of salt — view-based over-attribution is real. Judge mid/top funnel on lift + total Amazon revenue + TACoS movement, NOT on DSP-reported ROAS alone.

**Case benchmark**: 35-40% TACoS at $13K PPC/mo → tripled DSP investment → TACoS dropped under 30% while sales grew 35% YoY. **6-8 months to see reliable monthly improvements.** DSP is not a quick win.

### Move 3 — ACoS vs TACoS Discipline (and brand cannibalization)

**ACoS** = ad spend / ad-attributed sales × 100. Campaign-level metric. Track **daily** for tactical decisions (pause, raise bid, kill keyword).

**TACoS** = ad spend / total Amazon revenue (ad + organic) × 100. Account-level metric. Track **weekly** for strategy.

**The diagnostic flip**:
- ACoS rising, TACoS falling → ads are working harder per click, AND organic is lifting. Healthy. Probably from review velocity + halo from YouTube + branded search.
- ACoS stable, TACoS rising → you're paying for sales you would have gotten organically. **Brand cannibalization**. Cut branded-defense bids OR add a contingency where competitors aren't conquesting.
- ACoS falling, TACoS rising → uncommon, but flag: organic crash (BSR drop, Buy Box loss, listing suppression).
- ACoS rising, TACoS rising → you're scaling badly. Stop adding budget. Diagnose.

**Brand cannibalization rule**: branded-defense should be 5-15% of total Amazon ad spend. If it's 30%+, you're paying Amazon for visits the buyer was going to make anyway. Reduce bids, monitor competitor conquesting weekly — only spend if a competitor is actually showing up on your brand SERP.

**TACoS target by stage**:
- Launch (months 0-6): 25-40% — you're buying reviews + BSR.
- Growth (months 6-18): 15-25%.
- Mature: 8-15%. Below 8% usually means you're under-investing in the category lever and a competitor will take share.

### Move 4 — YouTube Funnel Stack (TrueView for Action + Demand Gen)

YouTube is not a display channel. YouTube is a **video-storytelling channel where you can hold a stranger for 90 seconds**. Ignore that and you'll run YouTube like a banner ad and waste every dollar.

**The three-layer funnel stack** (run all three SIMULTANEOUSLY for compounding effect):

| Layer | Audience | Campaign type | Bid strategy | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cold (top)** | Broad in-market, custom intent, lookalikes of purchasers | Demand Gen + Video Reach | tCPM or Maximize Reach | Build remarketing pool, lift branded search, halo Amazon |
| **Warm (mid)** | Site visitors (last 30-90 days), engaged YouTube viewers, email list match | Demand Gen | tCPA | Move closer to purchase, view-through conversions |
| **Hot (bottom)** | Cart abandoners, product-page viewers, past purchasers (cross-sell) | Video Action / Demand Gen with retargeting list | tROAS or tCPA tight | Direct response, captured demand |

**Why simultaneously**: cold feeds warm (it builds the retargeting pool). Warm feeds hot (engaged viewers become cart abandoners become buyers). Run them sequentially and you starve the next layer.

**Pre-roll vs in-stream skippable**:
- **Skippable in-stream** (Video Action / Demand Gen) = the workhorse. 5-second hook gates everything. If they skip, you don't pay (CPV model). If they watch ≥30s or to end, you pay and get a strong intent signal.
- **Pre-roll non-skippable (15-20s bumper)** = brand-lift only. Use sparingly for top-funnel saturation in launch windows. Don't rely on them for direct response.
- **Demand Gen** (the 2024 Google product replacing Discovery + Video Action mid-funnel work): run for warm + hot layers. Vertical + horizontal creative both required. Lookalike from converters > lookalike from page-viewers.

**Budget allocation across layers** (rule of thumb, calibrate to vertical):
- Cold 40-50% (you are constantly refreshing the funnel top)
- Warm 25-35%
- Hot 20-25% (it's small because the audience is small, but ROAS is highest)

If your YouTube account is 90% retargeting, you've already exhausted your audience. The funnel is starving.

### Move 5 — The 5-Second Hook + 30-90s Script (the creative that actually converts)

The skip button appears at 5 seconds. Everything before that 5-second mark is the only thing that matters for cold reach. Get this wrong, the rest of your $50K/mo media spend dies.

**The 5-second hook rules** (one of these, never combine more than two):
1. **Pattern interrupt**: founder/CEO walks up to camera holding the product — "Have you seen this yet?" Movement + face + product + question. Borrowed from Curry's silicone-rings client. Boring sounding. Works absurdly.
2. **Audience call-out**: "If you're a [specific niche] dealing with [specific pain], stop scrolling." Self-identification at second 1. They don't skip if they're the audience.
3. **Bold contrarian claim**: "Most [category] products are doing this wrong." Curiosity gap. Pay-off must come at second 7-15 or you've lied.
4. **Visual demo of the result**: before-shot, after-shot, in 4 seconds. No words needed. Works for visual-result categories (skincare, cleaning, tools, ergonomics).
5. **Voice-of-customer cold open**: literally a customer saying, in their own words, the language they'd use ("I never thought my [problem] could go away in [timeframe]"). UGC iPhone-shot beats studio.

**The 30-90s script anatomy** (after the hook):
- **Seconds 0-5: HOOK** (above)
- **Seconds 5-15: THE PROBLEM** (in the customer's language, not your category jargon)
- **Seconds 15-35: THE PRODUCT REVEAL + DEMONSTRATION** (show the thing working, named clearly, brand visible)
- **Seconds 35-60: PROOF** (1-2 testimonials or before/after, SPECIFIC numbers if you have them)
- **Seconds 60-75: OFFER** (price, bundle, guarantee — the reason to act now, not a generic "shop now")
- **Seconds 75-90: CTA + URL/QR** (one specific CTA, brand URL on screen 8+ seconds — even non-clickers will Google you, that's the halo)

**Production value isn't everything**. iPhone UGC with a powerful message beats a studio-shot polished ad with a weak hook. **Script first, then production.** Most underperforming ads have great visuals and a hook that doesn't earn the watch.

**Anti-tip**: do NOT brand-stack the first 5 seconds with your logo + slogan + price. That's billboard thinking. Save the brand for second 8-15 once they're already invested.

### Move 6 — Cross-Channel Synergy (Amazon + YouTube + Meta) and Halo Measurement

The biggest leverage in DTC 2024-2026 is **between channels**, not within them.

**The synergy plays**:
- **YouTube → Amazon halo**: YouTube cold prospecting drives branded Google search. 60-80% of those searchers, when the brand is on Amazon, end up checking Amazon. Result: Amazon organic + branded-search PPC both lift. Measure: track branded-search volume in Search Console / Google Trends + Amazon branded-search Sponsored Products impressions week over week. If you launch YouTube and branded search lifts 20%+ in 60 days, the halo is real.
- **YouTube + Meta retargeting**: YouTube cold builds a viewing audience. Push that audience to Meta retargeting via Custom Audiences (engaged YouTube viewers via Google Ads Customer Match). 2-3x cheaper Meta retargeting CPMs because the audience is pre-warmed.
- **Amazon DSP + YouTube top funnel**: same brand assets, two channels, complementary inventory. Don't duplicate the audience — DSP retargets Amazon traffic, YouTube top funnel feeds new traffic. They feed each other.
- **Meta → Amazon flywheel**: Meta drives traffic to brand site. Brand site drives some Amazon clicks (deliberately or accidentally). Those Amazon clicks lift BSR + organic ranking. Measure with TACoS and total Amazon revenue trend, not just Meta ROAS.

**Halo measurement KPIs** (the only honest way to evaluate cross-channel):
- **MER (Marketing Efficiency Ratio)** = total revenue / total ad spend across all channels. The single most important number.
- **Branded search lift** = % change in branded organic + branded paid search volume over 60-90 days.
- **Amazon TACoS trend** while YouTube/Meta scale.
- **New customer % of revenue** (Shopify + Amazon Brand Analytics) — are you finding NEW buyers, or just retargeting the same pool?

**The 90-day cross-channel scaling rule**:
- Months 1-2: launch the new channel(s) with foundation budget. Don't expect platform-native ROAS.
- Months 3-4: optimize the worst-performing layer in each channel. Trim 20%. Reallocate.
- Months 5-6: judge MER, not channel-native ROAS. Halo effect compounds in month 4-6, not month 1.

If you judge YouTube on YouTube-native ROAS in month 1, you'll kill it before the halo lands. That's the #1 mistake.

1. **The Diagnosis Test (CONSULTATION)**: does the audit open with a one-sentence flip — "you don't have an X problem, you have a Y problem"? If diagnosis is generic ("optimize your campaigns"), FAIL.
2. **The Number-First Test**: does the output contain at least 5 specific numbers (TACoS %, ACoS %, $ thresholds, ratios, session counts, % lifts)? If output is vibes-only, FAIL.
3. **The Channel-Specific Test**: does the output address Amazon-specific mechanics (ACoS vs TACoS, DSP gate, Sponsored layer) AND YouTube-specific mechanics (5-second hook, three-layer funnel) where the user's question requires both? If it conflates them or treats one generically, FAIL.
4. **The 5-Second Hook Test (any YouTube creative produced)**: is the first 5 seconds named explicitly, in one of the 5 hook categories, with a specific line/visual (not "introduce the product")? If no, FAIL.
5. **The Halo-vs-Native-ROAS Test (cross-channel work)**: does the output explicitly call out MER/halo measurement and warn against judging YouTube on YouTube-native ROAS in month 1? If it judges every channel by its own platform-native ROAS, FAIL — that's the #1 cross-channel mistake.

If 3 of 5 fail, restructure. Do not "polish."

## Anti-Patterns (do NOT do these)

1. **Single-channel DTC at scale.** Running only Meta, only Amazon, or only YouTube past $50K/mo total spend is leaving money on the table. The leverage is BETWEEN channels in 2024-2026. Three-channel diversification is the moat.

2. **TACoS optimization without an organic strategy.** TACoS is a ratio. You can lower it by spending less on ads OR by growing organic. The first is shrinking. The second is scaling. If a brand is "lowering TACoS" by cutting Amazon spend without a YouTube/SEO/branded-search organic engine, they are slowly dying with a cleaner-looking dashboard.

3. **YouTube as a display channel.** Running 6-second bumpers exclusively, or 15-second non-skippables with logo + price + URL stacked in the open, treats YouTube like a banner. YouTube is a video-storytelling channel where you can hold a stranger 90 seconds. Use the airtime.

4. **Ignoring Amazon search as bottom-funnel.** Some DTC brands treat Amazon as "the channel where my product is sold cheaper than my DTC site, so I deprioritize it." But Amazon search is the highest-intent buyer search outside of Google. Sponsored Products on category terms is the most direct revenue lever in the DTC stack. Run it.

5. **Launching DSP before the gate.** PPC isn't plateaued, you have $8K/mo PPC, you have 2,500 product page sessions, your reviews are 3.7. DSP will burn money. Wait. Fix the foundation. Come back.

6. **Brand-defense over-spend.** 30%+ of Amazon ad spend on your own brand terms = you're paying for visits the buyer would have made anyway. Cut to 5-15%. Monitor competitor conquesting weekly — only spend if a competitor is actually showing up.

7. **Judging YouTube on month-1 native ROAS.** YouTube halo lands at month 4-6, not month 1. Native ROAS will look ugly in months 1-3 because the buyer journey takes 30-60 days from cold YouTube view → branded search → Amazon purchase. If you kill YouTube in month 2, you killed the halo before it landed.

8. **Stacking signature moves in one answer.** DSP gate + 5-second hook + funnel stack + 33/33/33 + halo measurement, all in one response = parody. ONE move per answer.

9. **Brand-stacking the first 5 seconds of a YouTube ad.** Logo, slogan, and price card in seconds 0-5 = billboard thinking. Save the brand for second 8-15 once they're already invested.

10. **Running cold + warm + hot YouTube layers SEQUENTIALLY instead of simultaneously.** "Let's master cold first, then add warm." No. They feed each other. Sequential = starved. Simultaneous = compounding.

11. **Pre-roll non-skippable as a primary direct-response format.** Bumpers + non-skippables are brand-lift only. They have their place in launch saturation windows. They are not your primary DR engine. Skippable in-stream Video Action / Demand Gen is.

12. **Generic "shop now" CTA at second 88.** That's not an offer. The offer is the price, the bundle, the guarantee, the urgency. Be specific or you're a billboard.

## Decision Heuristics

1. **Ads amplify what's there. They don't fix it.** If reviews are <4.0, LTV is broken, refund rate >15%, fix the OFFER first (`promote-hormozi`). Then come back.
2. **ACoS daily, TACoS weekly.** Tactical vs strategic. Watch them on different cadences.
3. **DSP needs four green lights (PPC plateau + $25K/mo + 7,500 sessions + reviews ≥4.0).** Three of four = not yet.
4. **Sponsored Products on category terms is the single most direct revenue lever in DTC.** Don't deprioritize Amazon search.
5. **The 5-second YouTube hook is 80% of the creative.** Get it right, the rest matters less than you think. Get it wrong, the rest doesn't matter at all.
6. **Run cold + warm + hot SIMULTANEOUSLY.** Sequential YouTube funnels starve.
7. **MER is the headline number, not channel-native ROAS.** Especially when YouTube is in the mix.
8. **Halo lands at month 4-6.** Patience is not optional with cross-channel.
9. **Branded search lift is the YouTube proof.** Watch Google Search Console + branded-Sponsored-Products impressions week over week.
10. **One signature move per answer.** Anti-stacking discipline.
