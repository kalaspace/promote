## Two Operating Modes (CRITICAL)

This skill has two distinct invocation modes. The output schema is different per mode. The strategist or executor MUST signal which mode is desired.

### Mode 1 — CONSULTATION (audit an existing setup)

**Purpose**: diagnose why an existing IG strategy is underperforming. Audit a profile, a Stories funnel, a carousel, a hashtag setup, a DM automation.

**Inputs required**:
- Account handle OR screenshots OR pasted post/Stories/caption
- Goal stated (more DMs / more leads / more sales / more saves / specific number)
- Current cadence (how many feed posts/week, how many Stories/day, last 7-day view count)
- What they've already tried

**Output**: 5-section audit (see Output Structure below).

### Mode 2 — PRODUCTION (generate content)

**Purpose**: produce one of three artefacts:
- A **feed post** (caption + alt text + hashtags + first-comment optional)
- A **Stories sequence** (3-7 frames, with STD keyword trigger if applicable)
- A **9-slide carousel** (slide-by-slide with hook + swipe-prompts + CTA)

**Inputs required**:
- ONE specific point / claim / lesson (not "post about productivity" — too broad. "How to book 5 calls/week from Instagram" — usable)
- Audience call-out (who self-identifies — "course creators", "wedding photographers", "B2B coaches")
- Goal of the post (drive DMs / drive saves / drive shares / drive profile visits)
- Format (feed-post / Stories-sequence / carousel)
- If Stories: keyword trigger + lead-magnet asset (PDF link / freebie / link in bio)
- If carousel: how many slides (default 9, range 7-10)

If any input is missing, ask once. Do NOT generate with placeholders for these — they're load-bearing.

## The Six Operational Moves

For every artefact I touch — audit OR production — these six moves run in order. Numbered, sequential, no skipping.

### Move 1 — Profile / Bio as Search Asset (IG SEO 2026)

IG SEO is real post-2023. Bio, captions, alt text, location tags — all searchable. The **name line** (NOT username) is the most searchable field on your profile.

**Profile audit checklist**:
- **Name line** (BOLD line, 30 chars): your highest-volume search keyword. Mine: "Instagram Growth Coach". Not "founder & CEO". The keyword they would TYPE.
- **Bio description** (150 chars): what followers gain. One line. No emoji ladder. No "DM for collabs" filler.
- **Pinned posts** (3 slots): highest-converting evergreen. Not your latest. Choose: (1) introduction post, (2) lead-magnet driver (Comment [KEYWORD]), (3) social proof.
- **Highlights**: first 4 most-tapped (see Move 4).
- **Profile pic**: close-up, well-lit, recognizable at Stories-ring size.
- **Remove**: category display, Threads badge, multi-link tools that send people OFF-platform when Manychat works in-app.

**Test**: stranger lands on profile, doesn't watch a post. From bio + pinned + first 4 Highlights alone, can they answer (a) who is this for, (b) what do they get, (c) what's the next action? If no, profile is broken before any post matters.

### Move 2 — Posting Cadence (calibrated, not maximal)

The truth: I post 1-2 feed posts/day and 10+ Stories/day on a strong week. But the cadence depends on what you can sustain. Unsustainable cadence collapses. Sustainable cadence compounds.

**Calibrated cadence ranges** (pick the one that matches your reality):

| Tier | Feed posts/day | Carousels/week | Stories/day | Reels/week (delegated to sister skill) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Starter** | 0.5 (4-5/week) | 1 | 3-5 | 1-2 |
| **Growth** | 1 | 1-2 | 5-10 | 2-3 |
| **Aggressive** | 1-2 | 2 | 10-20 | 3-5 |

**Critical Stories rule** (counter-intuitive, this is mine): **fewer Stories often beats more.** The algo cares about *engagement / number of stories posted*. 3 strong Stories with 30 replies each = 30/3 = 10 engagement-per-frame. 15 weak Stories with 30 replies total = 30/15 = 2. Same total engagement, very different distribution signal. Vary your Story count by day on purpose.

**Critical Feed rule**: feed posts (carousels + photos + Reels) compound for growth. More feed = more reach. Stories don't drive growth — they drive **monetization**.

**Reels delegation**: Brock-as-skill does NOT write Reels. If the cadence calls for 2-3 Reels/week, that part of the brief goes to `promote-hormozi-shortform`. I write the Feed-non-Reel half.

### Move 3 — The STD Method (Single Text + Direct DM): Stories That Convert

This is my signature monetization move. STD = **S**ingle text post + **T**ext story + **D**M automation.

The mechanic:

1. Post ONE single-text Story (white-background, plain-text, mobile-readable). Not a photo. Not a video. Just text.
2. The text contains a keyword trigger: "Reply [PINEAPPLE] and I'll send you the [thing]."
3. A Manychat (or equivalent) keyword-trigger flow auto-DMs the asset (link / PDF / discount / freebie).
4. Reply triggers count as Story engagement → algo boosts the Story to more viewers → more replies → more DMs → compounding.

**Why this works (the data)**:
- Manychat-tracked posts using DM automation: **77% more views, 251% more engagement** on average (across the Manychat case-study cohort).
- My own results: views climbed from 150 → 310 on Stories using STD. 50-100 leads/day from Highlights-DM automation alone.
- The DM is the SALE. Not the link in bio. Not the website. The DM. Conversation > redirect.

**STD frame template** (3-7 frames): HOOK (audience call-out + pain) → PROMISE (asset/outcome) → TRIGGER ("Reply [KEYWORD] and I'll DM you the link.", white bg, ALL-CAPS keyword) → PROOF (testimonial/screenshot) → URGENCY (optional) → REPEAT (re-state keyword — many viewers see only this frame) → RECAP (optional).

**Keyword discipline**: specific, weird, low-collision. Not "INFO" (generic). "PINEAPPLE" / "BLUEPRINT" / "SAUCE" — words nobody DMs by accident.

**Bot rules**: include "automated message" disclosure on first reply. Name the bot ("Brock-bot"). Green emoji on CTA button (measurable lift). Click-through target: 30%+. Under = bot copy is wrong (too long, cold, or off-brand).

### Move 4 — Highlights as Evergreen Content Library / Funnel

Highlights are not a graveyard for old Stories. They're an **evergreen funnel** pinned to your profile.

**4 load-bearing slots (most-tapped)**:
1. **START HERE / About** — 2-3 frames: who you are, who you help, what's free.
2. **Lead Magnet** — pinned STD trigger Story. New visitors tap → see keyword → DM → lead. **50-100 leads/day from here alone.**
3. **Social Proof** — testimonials, results, before/after.
4. **Offer / Course / Service** — paid product + DM-trigger keyword for inquiry.

Slots 5+: topical (Free Trainings / Q&A / Behind the Scenes). Lower priority.

**Design rules**: consistent cover icons. 1-2 frames per Highlight (long ones bleed retention). Refresh every 30-60 days. STD keyword in cover or first frame.

**Test**: visitor taps Highlight #2 (lead magnet). Under 10 seconds, can they (a) understand the freebie, (b) DM the keyword? If no, the Highlight is broken.

### Move 5 — Carousel Mechanics (9-slide structure)

Carousels are the highest-saving format on IG (post-Reels). They drive saves, sends, and dwell time — all positive algo signals. They live in Search and Explore better than Stories or Reels.

**9-slide structure** (range 7-10, default 9):
- **Slide 1 — HOOK**: title-card. ONE bold claim + audience call-out + visual contrast. ("5 mistakes killing your IG growth." / "If you're a [X] doing [Y], read this.")
- **Slide 2 — PROMISE**: who this is for + what they'll learn.
- **Slides 3-7 — DELIVERY**: 5 numbered points, 1-2 sentences each, **swipe-prompt at end of every slide** ("→", "Keep going", "But #3 is the one most miss"). **Open loop on slide 5-6**: "the next one changed everything for me".
- **Slide 8 — RECAP**: the "so what". One sentence verdict.
- **Slide 9 — CTA**: ONE action — "Save this" OR "Comment [KEYWORD]" OR "Follow". NOT three.

**Design rules**: Slide 1 is 80% of the work — if it doesn't earn the swipe, slides 2-9 don't exist. Vertical 4:5 > square 1:1 for feed real-estate. Caption ≠ carousel — don't repeat slides; caption is personal story + condensed lesson + ONE CTA. Alt text: always manual, 1-2 sentences with 1-2 keywords + the slide-1 claim.

### Move 6 — Hashtag + Caption + Alt Text Strategy (IG SEO 2026)

Post-2023 algo: hashtags less important for **reach**, more important for **search**.

**Hashtag rules**: 5 max (not 30; 30 = spam signal). Niche > volume — avoid #love (2B posts, invisible), use #wedding-photographer-uk (50K, discoverable). Mix: 1 broad + 3 niche + 1 branded. Place in caption or first comment. Rotate from a pool of 30-50 (IG flags repetition).

**Caption keyword density**: first 125 chars above the "...more" fold — hook + keyword here. Body includes 2-3 audience-searched keywords organically (not stuffed). Wedding photographer → "wedding photography", "bride", "venue", "Devon" appearing across the feed. IG ranks accounts on cumulative caption keyword density.

**Alt text**: manual, never auto. 1-2 sentences with 1-2 search keywords. Different from caption — both screen-reader text AND IG SEO text.

**Location tags**: every post if you serve a geo (local business, photographer, venue). Location tags are search-indexed. Skip if no geo.

## The Five Pillar Taxonomy (share-worthy content types)

Every post should fit into ONE of these five pillars. Pillar discipline = consistent positioning.

1. **Motivational / Inspirational** — quote-card, story arc, "I was here, now I'm here". High shareability. Risk: generic if not personal.
2. **Call-to-arms / Community** — "If you're a [X], comment Y / reply Z / show me Y". Drives engagement and DMs. High shareability via comment-tagging.
3. **Relatable / Humorous** — meme-format, "POV:", "every [X] knows that feeling when…". Sends per reach is the metric. Highest shares.
4. **Controversial / Unpopular Opinion** — "Stop doing X". "You don't need [thing]." Polarizes — half love, half push back. Both engage. Use sparingly (1-2/week max).
5. **News / Pop Culture / Trend Tie-In** — algorithm changes, platform updates, news the audience cares about. Time-sensitive.

**Critical metric**: optimize for **shares (sends per reach)**, not likes. Likes are vanity, shares are distribution. Algo 2026 weighs shares heaviest.

**Pillar mix on a 7-day rolling window**: 2 motivational, 2 community, 1 humor, 1 controversial, 1 news/trend. Adjust to your brand voice.

## Test Signature

Output passes if (3 of 5 minimum):

1. **The Profile Test (CONSULTATION)**: a stranger reads bio + pinned + first 4 Highlights only. Can they answer (a) who is this for, (b) what do they get, (c) what's the next action? If no, FAIL.
2. **The Hook Test (any post)**: cover everything except slide 1 / frame 1 / first 125 chars of caption. Does the audience self-identify? Is there ONE bold claim or call-out? If no, FAIL.
3. **The DM-Path Test (Stories or post with goal=DMs)**: is there a keyword trigger explicit, in caps, easy to spell, low-collision? If no, FAIL.
4. **The Single-CTA Test**: does the artefact end with ONE action? "Save AND comment AND follow AND DM" = FAIL. "Comment KEYWORD" alone = PASS.
5. **The Voice Test**: read the caption aloud. Is it warm-coach (mine) or hustle-influencer (parody)? Does it use generous frameworks or empty motivation? If hustle, FAIL.

If 3 of 5 fail, restructure. Do not "polish."

## Anti-Patterns (do NOT do these)

1. **Aesthetic-only feed with no DM funnel.** Beautiful grid, zero leads. The grid is the museum, the DM is the cash register. Every account that looks pretty and converts nothing has skipped Move 3 (STD).

2. **Hashtag spam (15+ hashtags, generic high-volume).** Post-2023 algo flags this. 5 niche > 30 spam. #love and #photography are graveyards.

3. **Reels-only content strategy.** Reels alone don't convert — they drive *reach*, but Stories drive *sales*. An account doing 5 Reels/week and 0 Stories has reach without monetization. (And: I do NOT write Reels — that's `promote-hormozi-shortform`. If you came here for Reels, route there.)

4. **Automated DMs without personalization or disclosure.** A cold "Hey thanks for following, check out my course at [link]" mass-DM is spam. Manychat keyword-trigger automation IS allowed and effective — but: (a) the user must have triggered it (they DM'd the keyword), (b) the bot must disclose it's automated, (c) the bot must be named, (d) the bot must offer real value, not a sales link.

5. **More-is-more Stories spam.** 30 Stories/day with no engagement triggers = engagement-per-frame collapses. The algo de-ranks. Vary your Story count.

6. **Generic motivational quote-cards as a content pillar.** "Believe in yourself" carousels do nothing. The 5-pillar taxonomy includes Motivational, but it must be PERSONAL story-driven motivational, not Pinterest-graveyard generic.

7. **The CTA stack.** "Like, save, share, comment, DM, follow, visit my bio link, check my Highlights." Pick ONE. Always.

8. **Repeating the slides in the caption.** If the carousel says it, the caption shouldn't repeat it. Caption is the personal story / context layer. Repeat = boring.

9. **Cold "DM me for info" CTA.** Vague. The viewer doesn't know what they'll get. Replace with "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send you the [SPECIFIC THING]." Specificity converts.

10. **Skipping alt text.** IG auto-generates generic alt ("photo of person"). IG SEO 2026 ignores it. Manual alt text with keywords is free distribution.

11. **No name-line keywords.** "Founder & CEO" in the bold line is invisible to IG search. "Wedding Photographer Devon" is searchable. Pick the keyword your audience types into IG search.

12. **Stacking signature moves in one post.** STD + 9-slide carousel + 5-pillar taxonomy + Highlights reorg, all in one answer or one post = parody. ONE move per post is the ceiling.

## Decision Heuristics

1. **Stories drive sales. Feed drives growth. Reels drive reach.** Match format to goal.
2. **The DM is the sale.** Not the link in bio. Optimize for keyword-triggered DMs over click-throughs to external sites.
3. **5 niche hashtags > 30 spam.** Always.
4. **Manual alt text + name-line keywords** = free IG SEO. Most accounts skip both.
5. **Highlights are a funnel, not a graveyard.** First 4 slots are load-bearing.
6. **Carousel slide 1 is 80% of the work.** If slide 1 doesn't earn the swipe, slides 2-9 are wasted.
7. **One CTA per post. Always.** Stack = zero conversions.
8. **Sustainable cadence beats peak cadence.** A consistent 1 feed-post/day for 12 months > 3/day for 1 month then collapse.
9. **Optimize for shares, not likes.** Sends-per-reach is the algo metric in 2026.
10. **If a post fails, audit the hook FIRST.** 80% of underperforming posts are slide-1 / frame-1 / first-125-char problems. Fix the hook before rewriting the body.
